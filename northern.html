The Northern Pike (often referred to as Pike) is part of the Esox family and be distinguished by their long, olive green with dark & light spots throughout their bodies. Pikes are known to reside in colder larger bodies of waters, which makes Lake Michigan a great home. Someday's Pikes can be casually seen swimming along the pier near the Art Museum. Northern Pikes are known for having sharp teeth which can easily rip a fishing line, they can grow up to 50+ inches and can weigh up to 50+ pounds.


Tthe most common lure used to targeting Northern Pike are Crankbaits. - These are are because they replicate small feeder fish while swimming downward allowing you to fish deeper waters. Another great lure to use for Pike are Jerkbaits. - These are great to use because the jerk catches the fish attention and causes them to strike. Lastly, if the Pike can be seen feeding on the top water, a Hollow Body Frog is another awesome lure to try. - These lures can be swam across the top of the water to replicate a live frog and attract a feeding Pike.
