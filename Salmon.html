Salmon belong in the Salmonidae family and can easily be disguised by their pink bodies. They can be found in both fresh and salt water, and will grow to be up to 50+ inches and can weigh over 100 pounds. Every year during the fall, thousands of Salmon can be spotted spawning in the shallow rivers of Milwaukee.


One of the most common baits to use for targeting Salmon are Salmon eggs which are commonly placed in a sack. - The scent and coloration of this bait is meant to mimic freshly laid Salmon eggs. Another common lure used to target Salmon is a spoon lure. - These lures are great because they stand out and mimic minnows in the rough Lake Michigan waters. Lastly, another great option to target Salon are Spinner baits. - Similar to spoon lures, Spinner baits are flashy and create a lot of vibrations while moving through the water to attract more fish.
